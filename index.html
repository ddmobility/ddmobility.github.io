Our Solution

Multiple sensors in cars, including cameras, LiDAR, radar, and motion sensors, enable hazard detection. Cameras capture visual data to identify potholes and flooding. LiDAR creates a 3D map of the surroundings, accurately detecting road damage. Radar senses water accumulation and estimates its depth. Motion sensors detect changes in vehicle movement. These sensors feed data to a centralized processing unit, which employs AI algorithms for hazard recognition and classification. The system can warn the driver, trigger safety measures, or adjust the vehicle autonomously to mitigate risks associated with potholes and flooding. Overall, sensor integration enhances road safety.

Global Perspective

The application of a hazard detection system with multiple sensors in cars has global significance. This global implementation has the potential to save lives, reduce injuries, and improve the overall efficiency of transportation systems worldwide.
